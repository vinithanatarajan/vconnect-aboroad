Creative Industries

Specialise in digital pre- and post-production, animation, film, workshop practice, creative IT, project management or creative leadership.

The programme is focused on industry projects as well as your own major project. Learn fundamental techniques in a range of creative fields: 3D media, art and design,drawing, illustration, painting, printmaking and photography.

Gain a greater understanding about media. Work with a mix of media from visual arts to motion graphics and information technology. Learn through collaborative projects and take part in public exhibitions.

Primary Industries

Prepare for study in subjects that involve anatomy and physiology. Learn about the systems of the body, how it moves and how the body repairs itself. Go on to study in Animal Care, Beauty Therapy or Exercise Science.

Recognise normal and abnormal conditions and behaviourof animals. Do work placements in veterinaryclinics. You will also develop business skills inareas such as reception services, sales and dealing with clients.

Do work experience with Wellington SPCA anddevelop your skills in animal handling. Build yourunderstanding of animal health, welfare, ethicsand safe working practices.

Services and Management Industries

Understand concepts and techniques of business.

Learn the roles and responsibilities of management, develop effective communication skills and practise business leadership techniques.

Includes substantial practical experience.

Social and Community services

Gain an understanding of the causes and effects of addictive behaviours.

Learn strategies to assess and treat those suffering from addictions and develop a client-centred approach in your practice. Identify and apply different approaches when conducting assessments of clients.

Understand the assessment, intervention, management and treatment of addiction disorders. Facilitate a motivational interviewing session and cognitive behavioural therapy session.

Manufacturing and Technology Industires

Gain beginner skills in automotive and mechanical engineering.

Learn about engines, panel and paint, machining, metal work and welding. Produce engineering sketches. You will learn in our engineering and automotive workshops. Go on to further study to build your skills.

Try out a range of trade skills including motor mechanics, carpentry and related building trades. Be well prepared for further study.

Courses in Construction and Infrastruction

Learn about quantifying projects, estimating building costs and materials, and tendering and administering contracts.

Start your career as an architectural designer or draughtsperson. Develop your technical drawing skills using computer-aided design (CAD) software. Understand architecture, building methods, materials and building code requirements then create architectural plans to build your portfolio.

Gain skills in high demand and get into a well-paid career. Many of our students get part-time work in the industry in their second year.
